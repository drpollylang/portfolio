About Me

I am a software developer and PhD graduate with an interest in using data-driven and computational modelling approaches to understand complex biological systems. My multi-disciplinary background combines biochemistry, environmental science, analytical chemistry, statistical analysis, data science, and software development, allowing me to bridge the gap between wet-lab research and computational analysis.

I have experience in applying data analysis and machine learning to research problems, from developing new software tools for the analysis of air quality data to the use of natural language processing models for extracting information relevant to accessibility. To achieve these ends, I independently taught myself skills in statistical and data analysis, machine learning, Python, R, SQL and relational database handling, shell scripting, Linux command line, and many other tools (see the Resume tab for details) to complement my background in chemistry, biochemistry, environmental Science and computer science. My personal interests have also led me to develop my understanding of biology with a focus on immunology, microbiology and molecular genetics.

I am excited about the prospect of bringing my skills and experience to bear on research to help uncover biological mechanisms that would have profound real-world impacts, and to continue developing my skills and knowledge to support this aspiration.
